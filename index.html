Logline

A guilt-ridden photojournalist attempts to summit Kanchenjunga as penance for his brother's death, but when he becomes stranded and left for dead in the Himalayas, his fight for survival becomes a journey toward self-forgiveness—leading him to a hidden monastery where his father's disappeared climbing partner holds the key to his redemption.

The Story

Act One: The Weight of the Dead

Arjun Mehta (32), a successful photojournalist, lives paralyzed by guilt. Five years ago, his brother Rohan died in a climbing accident—a trip Arjun skipped for work. Unable to commit to his girlfriend Meera or embrace life, Arjun decides to summit Kanchenjunga as suicidal penance.

Act Two: Descent Into Hell (6 Days)

Near the summit, Arjun's leg becomes trapped in ice. Left behind to save the group, he frees himself but descends the wrong mountain face into the restricted Indo-Chinese buffer zone. What follows is brutal: crevasse falls, collapsing ice bridges, hypothermia, starvation, hallucinations.

Act Three: Rebirth

Nearly dead, Arjun discovers a hidden monastery. The head Lama is Mahendra Singh—his father's disappeared climbing partner from 30 years ago. During six weeks of recovery, Lama Tenzin reveals he spoke to Rohan the day he died. Rohan's message: "Tell Arjun to stop punishing himself and live."

The Ending

One year later, Arjun returns—not to summit, but to volunteer at a memorial for missing climbers. He's married to Meera, expecting a child, missing three fingers. At the tree where he nearly died, he plants a prayer flag reading "LIVE." As the camera pulls back, another climber appears on the distant summit—the cycle continues, but Arjun has chosen life.

Risks & Mitigation

Production Risks

  • Weather-dependent shooting: Extended windows, backup locations
  • High-altitude safety: Experienced crew, medical team, comprehensive insurance
  • Budget overruns: Detailed prep, 10% contingency, local crew utilization
  • Permit/border zone challenges: Early government engagement, local co-producers

Market Risks

  • Niche genre appeal: Festival strategy builds prestige, platform deal reduces theatrical dependency
  • Dark subject matter: Marketing emphasizes transformation and hope over suffering
  • Unknown lead actor: Strong ensemble, director-driven marketing, "discovery" narrative
  • Regional specificity: Universal themes, English dialogue option, international co-production
